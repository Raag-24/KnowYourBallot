I. Before the Election (Preparation & Campaigning)

Eligibility Requirements: Clearly state the qualifications for running for office (age, residency, citizenship, etc.).

Nomination Process: Explain the process for getting on the ballot (filing deadlines, required paperwork, nomination signatures, etc.).

Campaign Finance Regulations: Detail all campaign finance laws, including contribution limits, expenditure limits, reporting requirements, and disclosure rules. This is CRUCIAL.

Code of Conduct: Outline ethical guidelines and standards of conduct for candidates during the campaign.

Campaigning Rules: Specify permissible and prohibited campaign activities (e.g., restrictions on campaigning near polling places, use of public resources, etc.).

Media Regulations: Explain rules related to advertising in various media (print, radio, TV, online). Include regulations on truth in advertising and disclaimers.

Debate Participation: If debates are organized, explain the rules for participation.

II. During the Election (Election Day)

Polling Place Regulations: Outline rules for candidate representatives or poll watchers at polling places. Include rules about access, conduct, and interaction with voters.

Challenging Voters: Explain the process for challenging a voter's eligibility (if applicable). Emphasize the importance of doing so fairly and within legal guidelines.

Prohibited Activities: Clearly state any prohibited activities on election day (e.g., campaigning inside polling places, offering inducements to voters).

III. After the Election (Post-Election)

Counting of Ballots: Briefly explain the ballot counting and verification process. Transparency is key.

Recount Procedures: Detail the rules and procedures for requesting a recount.

Election Challenges: Explain the process for formally challenging election results.

Reporting Requirements: Reiterate post-election campaign finance reporting requirements and deadlines.

IV. General Information

Candidate Rights: Clearly list and explain the rights of candidates. This includes freedom of speech (within legal limits), equal access to media (with fairness stipulations), and the right to a fair election.

Candidate Responsibilities: Outline the responsibilities of candidates, including ethical conduct, adherence to election laws, and accurate representation of information.

FAQs: Create a comprehensive FAQ section to address common candidate questions.

Contact Information: Provide contact information for relevant election officials or organizations.

V. Transparency and Accessibility

Public Disclosure: Emphasize the importance of transparency in campaign finance and candidate information.

Accessibility: Ensure that campaign materials and websites are accessible to people with disabilities.

VI. Legal Counsel

Disclaimer: Strongly advise candidates to consult with legal counsel specializing in election law for specific guidance on all rules and regulations. Election law is complex and varies by jurisdiction. Your website should not be a substitute for professional legal advice.

VII. Keeping Information Current

Regular Updates: Commit to keeping all information on the website up-to-date, especially regarding election laws and deadlines. This is critical.

By covering these areas, you can create a valuable resource for candidates, ensuring they are well-informed and compliant with all applicable rules and regulations. Remember to present the information in a clear, concise, and easy-to-understand manner. The complexity of election law necessitates the strong recommendation for candidates to seek legal counsel.
